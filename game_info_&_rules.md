# Love Letter — Info and Rules

## What It Is

Love Letter is a fast deduction card game by Seiji Kanai for 2–6 players. Players are suitors trying to get their letter delivered to the Princess by using people in her court. Each round is short: players draw one card, play one card, and try either to survive until the deck runs out or eliminate everyone else.

Core skills: deduction, bluffing, memory, risk management, and timing.

## Objective

Win favor tokens by winning rounds. A player wins a round by either:

1. Being the only player still in the round, or
2. Having the highest-value card in hand when the deck runs out.

The full game ends when a player reaches the required number of favor tokens:

| Players | Favor tokens to win |
|---:|---:|
| 2 | 6 |
| 3 | 5 |
| 4 | 4 |
| 5 | 3 |
| 6 | 3 |

Multiple players can win the game at the same time if they reach the winning token count simultaneously.

## Components

Current Z-Man edition:

- 21 character cards
- 6 reference cards
- 13 favor tokens
- 1 rulebook
- 1 cloth bag

## Setup

1. Give each player a reference card.
2. Shuffle the 21 character cards into a facedown deck.
3. Put the favor tokens within reach.
4. Set aside the top card of the deck facedown without looking at it.
5. In a 2-player game, also set aside 3 more cards faceup.
6. Deal 1 card to each player as their starting hand.
7. The player who most recently hand-wrote a letter takes the first turn.

## Turn Structure

Play proceeds clockwise.

On your turn:

1. Draw 1 card from the deck.
2. You now have 2 cards in hand.
3. Choose 1 card to play faceup.
4. Resolve that card's effect.
5. Keep the other card as your hand.

Played and discarded cards stay visible to all players. This matters because deduction depends on tracking what cards are already gone.

## Being Out of the Round

Some effects eliminate a player from the current round.

When you are out:

- Discard your hand faceup.
- Do not resolve the discarded card's effect.
- Skip all future turns until the next round.
- You cannot be chosen for card effects.
- You return normally at the start of the next round.

## End of a Round

A round ends in either of these cases:

### 1. Deck runs out

After a turn, if the deck is empty, all players still in the round reveal their hands. The highest-value card wins the round and gains 1 favor token.

If tied, all tied players win the round and each gains 1 favor token.

### 2. One player remains

If all players except one are out of the round, the remaining player immediately wins the round and gains 1 favor token.

## Starting the Next Round

To start the next round:

1. Shuffle all character cards again.
2. Set aside cards as in setup.
3. Deal 1 card to each player.
4. The previous round's winner starts.

If the previous round had multiple winners, choose randomly among them to determine who starts.

## Card Reference — Current Z-Man Edition

|---:|---|---:|---|
| 0 | Spy | 2 | No immediate effect. At the end of the round, if you are the only player still in the round who played or discarded a Spy, gain 1 extra favor token. This does not replace the normal round winner's token. You can gain only 1 Spy token per round. |
| 1 | Guard | 6 | Choose another player and name a character other than Guard. If that player has that card, they are out of the round. |

| 4 | Handmaid | 2 | Until the start of your next turn, other players cannot choose you for card effects. |

| 7 | King | 1 | Choose another player and trade hands with them. |
| 8 | Countess | 1 | No effect when played. You must play the Countess if the other card in your hand is the King or a Prince. You may also play her voluntarily. |

## Important Targeting Rules

Handmaid protects a player from being chosen by other players' card effects.

If every possible opponent is protected by Handmaid:

- Guard, Priest, Baron, and King are played with no effect because they require choosing another player.
- Prince must target yourself because it can choose any player, including yourself.

Players who are already out of the round cannot be targeted.

## Classic Version

The current edition can also be played in a classic 2–4 player mode.

Before setup, remove:

- 1 Guard
- 2 Chancellors
- 2 Spies

All other rules stay the same.

## Practical Strategy Notes

- Track visible cards. The game is small enough that memory matters.
- Guard gets stronger later in the round because fewer unknown cards remain.
- High-value cards are good at the end but dangerous if forced into comparison or discard effects.
- The Countess rule can reveal information indirectly: if someone plays Countess, they may have had King or Prince.
- Handmaid is strongest when you are holding a valuable card or when opponents have strong targeting effects.
- Prince is both offensive and defensive: it can remove an opponent's strong hand or save you from a weak one.
- Chancellor gives strong hand control and can hide information by returning cards to the bottom of the deck.
- Spy can create bonus-token pressure, but only if no other still-active player has played or discarded a Spy.

## Teaching Summary

Explain the game like this:

Each player always has one hidden card. On your turn, draw a second card, play one of the two, and resolve it. Played cards reveal information, and card effects can inspect, compare, protect, swap, or eliminate players. A round ends when the deck runs out or only one player remains. The round winner gains a favor token. First to the required number of favor tokens wins the game.
